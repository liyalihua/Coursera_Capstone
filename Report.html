Comparing the similarity of various neighborhoods and boroughs in Amsterdam based on the facilities around the neighborhoods using K-means

 

1.   Introduction

 

1.1  Background information

 

The capital of The Netherlands, Amsterdam was built in the in the 13th centuries, this is known as the old city center of Amsterdam. The first canal belt around the old city center was built in 1613 with booming economies and increasing inhabitants. Further expansion outside the canal rings took place during the golden age, in the 17th. After recovered from the wars and economies recession in the 18th century, the city expanded the canal network and modernize in the 19th century. The demographic development improved with colonial commerce and industrialization; these movements led to another expansion of the city in 19th century. At the beginning of 20th centrum, the old city center is surround by Amsterdam West, Amsterdam Oost, Amsterdam Noord and Amsterdam Zuid. The city is still expanding with a slower pace during the war time in the 20th century. The major urban expansion took place in Southeast of Amsterdam in the 1960s. Most recent development of the city is development of IJburg, six artificial islands on the IJmeer, located on the east side of the city.

 

1.2  Problem

 

The establishment of the division based on the historical geographical development as well as the urban planning development. The city of Amsterdam is divided into six parts: Centrum, West, New-West, South, East, North and Southeast. 4. K-mean

K-mean is an unsupervised machine learning algorithm, mainly for data exploration. It makes inference of the dataset by dividing the dataset into a certain number of clusters, given by k. We ran the model with K from 4 to 8, as the city is divided into seven parts, we only add one part as some area is still under construction.

When K = 4, it actually divided the city into 3 parts, which cluster 3 represent the city center/ vibrant area, cluster 2 represent the outskirts of the city and cluster 0 some neighborhoods which are close to a park.
